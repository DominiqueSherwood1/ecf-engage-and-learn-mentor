Your role as a mentor

Early career teachers (ECTs) now do a 2-year induction including a programme of coaching and mentoring based on the early career framework.

You’ll pay a really important part in this, helping support early career teachers develop the skills they need to thrive.

The programme has been produced by experienced teachers, school leaders, education experts and the Department for Education. It’s broken down into weekly topics aligned to the teachers’ standards.

Each topic offers teachers theory and practical classroom techniques that cover the early career framework and are aligned to the teachers’ standards.

We recommend you follow the order we’ve set them in where possible, but we’ve designed it to be flexible so you can switch things around if needed.

Your responsibilitie

A mentor should support early career teachers to:

  • integrate theory and practice so they can demonstrate they are meeting the teachers’ standards
  • ensure their well-being and their capacity to manage workload and maintain a work-life balance
  • develop their values and beliefs
  • model an ongoing commitment to question, reflect upon and develop practice in collaboration with colleague

You can do this by:

  • getting to know the study materials so you can get an idea of what your teachers will be learning
  • having regular meetings with your mentees to discuss issues and set goals
  • spending 10 to 15 minutes each week seeing how they put what they’ve learnt into practice
